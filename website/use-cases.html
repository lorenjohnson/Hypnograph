Use Cases

Not your dad's slideshow. Or is it?

Hypnograph is designed for reflective watching and serious composition work at the same time: reliable software behavior paired with intentionally surprising outcomes.

Who It Helps

Different audiences, one instrument.

Personal Reflection

Revisit your archive like an unfolding film.

Use Hypnograph to move through your own media as a living stream that supports deeper reflection on where you have been, where you are, and where you may be heading.

Family + Friends

Share memory experiences, not static albums.

Create expressive sequences that are easier to watch together and easier to talk through than a traditional folder-by-folder slideshow.

Content Creator

Rapid ad hoc compositions from your own footage.

Move from captured clips to stylized results quickly, with aspect ratio, speed, and rich effect-chain control in live preview.

Portfolio + Music Visuals

Produce stylized outputs with lower friction.

Build experimental visuals for songs, projects, and social formats without a heavyweight non-linear editing setup for every quick idea.

Group Work + Workshops

Run collaborative composition sessions.

Use shared source pools and facilitated workflows so participants can contribute media and collectively shape evolving outputs.

Live Visuals

Perform with decoupled preview and live output.

Keep exploring in preview while selectively sending the current hypnogram to a separate live display for projector and performance setups.

Fast Path

From phone capture to final output in minutes.

  1. Shoot on your phone and let it sync into Apple Photos.
  2. Open in Hypnograph, choose aspect ratio, speed, and effects while watching live preview.
  3. Export video for sharing, or save as a lightweight hypnogram reference for later re-render.

Library Workflow

Apple Photos curation as part of composition.

Favorites and exclusions stay actionable.

Mark favorites and exclusions during creative sessions, then review those outcomes directly in Apple Photos-integrated workflows.

Designed for evolving archive management.

Use composition passes to identify what to keep, what to revisit, and what to retire as your library changes over time.
