What Is Hypnograph

Hypnograph is a macOS visual instrument for working with your own photo and video archive in real time. It can begin in a generative mode, where surprising combinations emerge quickly, and it can also be steered into deliberate composition through clip selection, timing, layering, blend modes, and effect chains.

The point is not to generate new synthetic media. The point is to re-encounter material that already belongs to your life and shape it into something that feels newly alive.

In practice, Hypnograph sits between reflective viewing and creative editing: useful when you want to move fast from raw footage to expressive output, and useful when you want to dwell in discovery and notice what kinds of sequences, textures, and rhythms feel meaningful.

Collaborators

Hypnograph is at a turning point where I need to sharpen what the app actually is by grounding it in real use, not only in internal iteration. The immediate need is to involve a small set of collaborators who can work with what already exists, use it seriously, and reflect back what feels meaningful, confusing, exciting, or missing in expected behavior.

In this stage, collaborator means user-testing collaborator, not code contributor. The focus is less on bug-hunting as a standalone activity and more on careful rounds of use and feedback: what people try to do with the app, what they expect it to do, what it already does well, and where the product scope should narrow or deepen.

The use cases below are the working foundation for that process. They help identify who to invite, what kinds of sessions to run, and what product direction to prioritize while keeping authorship coherent and open to new ideas from people whose use feels relatable and concrete.

Use Case 1: Create Shorts for Social Media

Scenario

Start from a recently shot clip in Apple Photos (captured on iPhone, synced to Photos on Mac via iCloud), bring it into Hypnograph, process it with effects/montage, and export a short experimental clip for Instagram Story/Reel style posting.

What Already Works Well

  • Clip length can be determined globally.
  • A specific section of source clip can be selected and used.
  • Multiple effects can be stacked for rich output.
  • Multiple clips can be composited/montaged together.
  • Existing effects/effects engine already provides a strong range for experimental visual output.

Gaps

  • Per-layer rotation controls are missing. Rotate in 90-degree increments per layer would be useful.
  • Rendering multiple clips in sequence, including transitions between clips, is still missing as an integrated UI workflow.
  • No clear playback playhead indicator in layer scrubbing/timeline views.
  • No quick, intuitive frame-by-frame stepping workflow.
  • Missing a quick way (menu and/or keyboard based) for setting in/out points of clips or global range.
  • Global clip-length behavior is asymmetrical: reducing global length appears to reduce layer selection lengths, while increasing it leaves layer selections unchanged and can cause looping.
