Manifesto

Memory is fragile. Digital memory is absolute until it vanishes.

The digital paradox

You keep thousands of files that never age, then lose entire years during one device migration. The archive is either all there or gone.

The living album

A family photo from 1942 fades, curls, and carries time with it. Hypnograph brings that feeling of presence back to your digital media.

Surprise over search

You do not need another catalog to manage. You need a stream where forgotten moments can collide, return, and become meaningful again.

How it feels

Not a highlight reel. Not nostalgia theater. A raw channel into your own past.

Let it run

Open Hypnograph like turning on a TV and slip into your own footage without planning a narrative first.

Intervene in seconds

A scene flashes by. Rewind. Favorite it. Delete it. Keep moving. Shape the stream while staying inside the feeling.

Export the spark

When an unexpected sequence works, capture it instantly and share it as a clip that is yours, not AI-imagined.

Roadmap

From hypnotic stream to deliberate film output.

Near-term

Ship dependable timeline controls so multiple clip discoveries can be assembled and exported into one coherent movie.

Mid-term

Long-term

Expand into text and audio-responsive montage modes so archives can be recomposed as living audiovisual essays.
