# User Test: User (2026-03-31)

## Context
- Informal observational feedback rather than a hands-on use session.
- Feedback was recalled and recorded from memory on 2026-04-02.
- User did not directly operate the app during this round.

## Observations / Questions
- **“Why did you build this?”** was the most notable question.
  - This suggests the app’s purpose and emotional framing were not immediately legible from the product itself.
- **Initial reaction was that the app felt overwhelming.**
  - Part of this was tied to the more psychedelic effects visible during the demo.
  - Even after showing subtler examples, the overall impression still leaned toward “overwhelming.”
- **There was hesitation about using it on personal material.**
  - The user seemed concerned about applying the app’s treatment to their own photos and videos.

## Interpretation
- The app’s artistic intent and use case may not yet be self-evident to a new observer.
- The current visual tone can read as intense or intimidating, especially if the first effects shown are on the more psychedelic side.
- There may be value in framing Hypnograph more explicitly as something that can be used on:
  - a focused project
  - a dedicated album
  - or newly shot photos/videos gathered specifically for composition

## Takeaways
- The product story still needs to become more legible at first contact.
- A gentler or more clearly framed entry path may help users who are interested but hesitant.
- There may be onboarding value in showing that Hypnograph can be used with a constrained, project-specific set of media rather than requiring someone to throw their whole personal archive into it immediately.

## Potential follow-ups
1. Revisit how Hypnograph’s purpose is described in onboarding, marketing, or first-run surfaces.
2. Consider whether the initial effect presentation should bias toward calmer or more approachable examples.
3. Encourage future testers to try the app with a small, project-specific album or folder rather than their whole library.
